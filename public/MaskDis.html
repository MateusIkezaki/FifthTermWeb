Project's Description

With our return to campus(hopefully) just around the corner, we are sure people entering the Avenues campus will have to bring their own masks to campus. However, it's only natural that some will forget or lose their masks. With that in mind, we developed these mask dispensers that will be spread around the campus to those in urgent need of protection. All it takes is a quick swipe and you are good to go!


About our Project

We used an Ultrasonic Sensor to detect anything approaching the dispenser. Once the student's hand is close enough, the sensor will trigger the first Servo motor to rotate, dropping the first mask. Next time a student moves their hand under the sensor the sensor will rotate the next servo motor. This cycle will repeat until all masks have been dropped, thus requiring a refill.

The dispenser is shaped like a box and its interior is divided into levels, each level holding an individual mask. Each level is separated by a platform with little bumps on its top surface. These bumps will fit right into the gears attached to the servo motors. The gears will move the platform backwards, allowing the mask stored at said level fall right on the student's hand.

We do recognize, however, that this design is flawed. It can only hold up to 5 masks at a time, which is a pretty limited supply. That's why we are still working on new designs. We still need to talk to the head division about our idea, but if we manage to come up with an efficient design, this product would surely be very useful and would make the school's environment a lot safer.
